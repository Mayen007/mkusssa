About MKUSSSA

Building bridges and creating opportunities for South Sudanese students.

Our Story

MKUSSSA - Nairobi Campus was established in 2021 with a simple yet powerful vision: to foster South Sudanese unity on campus. Recognizing the need for a dedicated space where students could connect, share culture, and support one another, a group of passionate South Sudanese students established this campus chapter. In 2023, MKUSSSA was officially registered as a recognized student organization by Mount Kenya University, solidifying our commitment to serving the South Sudanese student community here at the Nairobi Campus.

Everything we do is guided by our core values: unity, inclusivity, academic excellence, good governance, integrity, and transparency. We believe in creating a space where every member is heard, valued, and empowered to succeed.

Our Mission

To strive for social cohesion, cultural diversity, good governance, and academic excellence for the betterment of our members and society.

Our Vision

To empower our members and promote peace, good leadership, skills, unity, and social development across the university community.

2022 - 2023

Founding Executive Board

MKUSSSA Nairobi’s founders served as the first executive board in 2022 - 2023, with Kuony Gatluak Thou as chairman. That team led the move from the MKUSSSA Thika structure to an autonomous MKUSSSA Nairobi chapter.

  • Chairman: Kuony Gatluak Thou
  • Deputy Chairperson: Ajoh Majak
  • Secretary General: Agok Mel
  • Finance Secretary: Atong Deng Nyibek
  • Information Secretary: Aquila Nyok

Their leadership marked the transition to an independent campus body.

2021 – 2023

Founding Period

The founding period began in 2021, when MKUSSSA Nairobi was established under MKUSSSA Thika. It continued through the 2022 - 2023 founding executive board, when the association claimed autonomy and stood as its own chapter.

This was the period when the chapter moved from shared oversight to self-governance and a distinct identity.
